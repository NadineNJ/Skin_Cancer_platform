Learn More About These Diagnoses

Melanoma

A serious and potentially life-threatening form of skin cancer that begins in the melanocytes (pigment-producing cells). It can spread quickly to other parts of the body if not treated early.

  • Often appears as a new or changing mole with asymmetry or uneven borders
  • May show multiple colors (brown, black, red, or even white)
  • Can develop on any part of the skin, even those not exposed to the sun
  • Requires immediate medical evaluation and possible biopsy

Pigmented Benign Keratosis (PBK)

A non-cancerous skin lesion that can resemble melanoma due to its pigmentation. It's a type of seborrheic keratosis, commonly seen in older adults and generally harmless.

  • Warty, waxy, or scaly surface, often dark or multi-toned
  • Typically does not change much over time
  • Common on the trunk, face, or scalp
  • Treatment is usually unnecessary unless irritated or for cosmetic reasons
